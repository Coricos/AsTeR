About Aster

Aster is an open source project that was started by UC Berkeley students on April 27th 2019. During the IBM Call for Code hackathon at UC Berkeley, we decided to build a disaster manager platform to dispatch the right resources at the right time given as many inputs as there are available.

Forest fires are frequent in California and natural disasters rise in intensity due to climate change. Ageing technologies used in unit dispatching struggle to keep the pace and do not allow efficient over-time monitoring and scalability. This is the context in which AsTeR was born.

Our Story

We are a group of passionnate engineers who began to work together at UC Berkeley. The different backgrounds of our team give us the ability to find helpful and powerful ideas to develop. We know what it’s like to have epic enthusiasm and inspiration for a tech concept and we are extremely pleased to bring it to life. From our different paths, it's always enjoyable to work as a team and share the talent and the skills with a significant purpose.

What gives us the will to work on this project? We were all chocked by the enormous fires in Califonia in 2018 and by the hurricane Irma in 2017. When you see these natural disasters, as young engineers, we wanted to be able to help the victims, the emergency services and all persons impacted by these catastrophes. The IBM Call for Code hackathon has been the perfect place to brainstrom and implement our idea of dispatching platform. Building a impactful and meaningful solution to help emergency services reflects exactly our way of thinking today and what we want to accomplish tomorrow.
