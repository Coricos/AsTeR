About Aster.

Aster is an open source project that was started by UC Berkeley students on April 27th 2019. Forest fires are frequent in California and natural disasters rise in intensity due to climate change. Ageing technologies used in unit dispatching struggle to keep the pace and do not allow efficient over-time monitoring and scalability. This is the context in which Aster was born.

During the IBM Call for Code hackathon at UC Berkeley, we decided to build a disaster manager platform to dispatch the right resources at the right time given as many inputs as there are available. Those inputs include for example:

    Emergency calls to 911 which are analyzed in real-time. Through speech-to-text technology, we extract insights about the problem, we quantify the priority and detect the keywords related to the issue for further control.

    Pictures taken on the field by the rescue team. By passing them through image classification, encountered problems are automatically detected. It allows us to update the dispatching map (graph) in real-time, which is exploited via a Dijkstra algorithm for optimal trajectories determination.
