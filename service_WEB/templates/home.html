Call Analysis

The AsTeR experience begins with the analysis of emergency calls to 911. We use IBM Watson speech-to-text technology and start-of-the-art artificial intelligence to turn oral calls into text and compute a priority score.

Unit Dispatching

AsTeR was designed to help and support firefighters and emergency teams in the field. By leading them in the most efficient way, the mission of help responders can be carried on even more securely than before.

The dispatching routes provided by AsTeR are built using so-called "Djikstra algorithms". These are efficient ways of computing optimal trajectories from one location to another. The trajectories can be updated in real time to continuously support the dispatched team, even in the most difficult situations.

Units also share their location in real time. This allows call centers to quickly visualise both overlaps and gaps in terms of help responder coverage.

Smart Feedback

AsTeR ensures that dispatched teams and call centers communicate as quickly and as efficiently as possible. Most of the information sent back to the call centers is generated automatically -- first responders are only asked to confirm it. Here are a few of the critical situations in which AsTeR will prove to be a game changer :

Blocked routes

In the event where an emergency vehicle encounters an obstacle on the road, the information is immediately sent back to AsTeR's database and all other vehicle trajectories are updated to avoid the obstruction.

New victims

Deployed units have the possibility to immediately notify the call center if they come across an additional victim. The location will be saved in the database and shared with all other units.

Image classification

First responders have the possibility to automatically share pictures taken on the field. These are then automatically classified using artificial intelligence, and can be used to update the dispatching map, or simply share provide more information to other units. This functionality could also be implemented on on-site drones.
